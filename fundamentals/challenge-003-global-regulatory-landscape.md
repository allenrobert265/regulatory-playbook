# 🌍 Introduction

Medical devices are sold all over the world, but there is no single global regulator that approves every medical device.

Instead, each country or region has its own regulatory authority responsible for ensuring that medical devices entering its market are safe, effective, and meet legal requirements.

Although many regulatory systems share similar principles, each has its own laws, classification systems, submission requirements, and approval pathways.

For medical device companies, understanding these regulatory systems is essential before entering a new market.

# 🇮🇳 India – CDSCO

**Full Form**

Central Drugs Standard Control Organization

### Responsibilities

- Regulates medical devices in India
- Classifies medical devices
- Issues licenses and approvals
- Conducts inspections
- Monitors post-market safety
- Enforces Medical Device Rules, 2017

---

# 🇺🇸 United States – FDA

**Full Form**

Food and Drug Administration

### Responsibilities

- Regulates medical devices sold in the United States
- Reviews submissions such as 510(k), De Novo, and PMA
- Conducts inspections
- Monitors recalls and adverse events
- Enforces Quality System Regulations

---

# 🇪🇺 European Union – EU MDR

Unlike India or the US, the European Union does not have one single approval authority.

Instead:

- Manufacturers comply with EU MDR 2017/745.
- Independent organizations called Notified Bodies assess many higher-risk devices.
- National Competent Authorities oversee enforcement within each member state.

Products that meet the requirements may receive CE marking, allowing them to be marketed within the European Economic Area.

---

# 🇬🇧 United Kingdom – MHRA

After Brexit, the UK established its own regulatory system.

MHRA is responsible for:

- Device registration
- Market surveillance
- Safety monitoring
- Regulatory guidance

---

# 🇦🇺 Australia – TGA

The Therapeutic Goods Administration regulates medical devices in Australia.

Responsibilities include:

- Device registration
- Conformity assessment
- Market surveillance
- Enforcement

---

# 🇨🇦 Canada – Health Canada

Health Canada regulates medical devices through the Medical Devices Regulations.

Responsibilities include:

- Licensing
- Device classification
- Safety monitoring
- Inspections

---

# 🇯🇵 Japan – PMDA

The Pharmaceuticals and Medical Devices Agency reviews medical devices before marketing in Japan.

Responsibilities include:

- Scientific review
- Safety evaluation
- Post-market monitoring

---

# 🌍 Why Are Global Regulations Different?

Different countries have:

- Different healthcare systems
- Different legal requirements
- Different risk classifications
- Different submission processes

Manufacturers must comply with the regulations of every country where they intend to sell their device.

---

# 🤝 International Harmonization

Although regulations differ, organizations work to make requirements more consistent worldwide.

Examples include:

- International Medical Device Regulators Forum (IMDRF)
- International Organization for Standardization (ISO)

These organizations help reduce differences between regulatory systems.

# 💡 Key Takeaways

- Every country has its own regulatory framework.
- Companies must comply with local regulations before marketing a device.
- Regulatory Affairs professionals often work with multiple regulatory systems.
- Global harmonization helps simplify international medical device regulation.
